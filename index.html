WHO WAS DOOM?

A UNIQUE VILLAIN

MF DOOM is considered one of the most celebrated and influential musicians in hip hop. Thom Yorke, who collaborated with MF DOOM, wrote: "He was a massive inspiration to so many of us, changed things... For me the way he put words was often shocking in its genius." Another rapper, Stereogum, noted MF DOOM's "formative" influence on younger rappers.

EVIL GENIUS: HOLORIMES

DOOM is unique because of his consistent use of multisyllabic rhyming phrases. "DOOM designed his songs to be a cascade of rhymes internally, and used external rhyme to connect lyrics. Part of the charm was DOOM's use of the 'Holorime' or an entire line or punchline that rhymes internally or externally within the bar.

DOOM's DISCOGRAPHY

OPERATION: DOOMSDAY

1998

MF DOOM's return to rap started with a bang, with a primarily self produced album, Operation Doomsday. It features dense rhyme schemes over tracks composed from a collage of R&B and cartoon samples.

MADVILLAINY

2004

Madvillainy received critical acclaim for it's production and lyricism, and is regarded as DOOM's magnum opus. The producer, Madlib, created most of the instrumentals in a hotel room using minimal equipment: a sampler, a turntable, and a tape deck. While Madvillainy achieved only moderate commercial success, it became one of the best-selling Stones Throw albums. It peaked at number 179 on the US Billboard 200, and attracted attention from media outlets not usually covering hip hop music, including The New Yorker.

DOOM'S LIFE

A VILLIAN ORIGIN STORY

In his youth, MF DOOM represented one-third of New York rap trio KMD under the moniker Zev Love X. The group consisted of his younger brother Dingilizwe, who performed as DJ Subroc, and their friend Jade 1. Unfortunately, shortly before the scheduled release of their second album, Black B*stards, DJ Subroc was hit by a car and killed.

Understandably, Zev Love X disappeared from the rap scene. After a number of years he resurfaced in 1998, assuming his final form: MF DOOM. After his hiatus, DOOM chose to hide his identity, donning his trademark mask.

A LASTING LEGACY

On December 31, 2020, DOOM's wife announced on social media that he had died.
The cause of death was not announced. DOOM will be forever remembered for his music and contributions to the genre of rap. There was a reflective thoughtfulness that went into producing a DOOM track. Each element, the lyricism, the rhymes, the entendres, the samples and the tracks all coalesce into a masterful work of art.
